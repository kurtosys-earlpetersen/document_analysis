Manager Commentary & Market Context

Monthly insights from the investment team

Market environment: February saw a modest pullback in US equities as investors digested mixed economic signals. The S&P 500 retreated amid concerns over persistent inflation readings and rising trade policy uncertainty. High-yield spreads widened slightly but remained near historical tights, reflecting resilient corporate fundamentals despite the macro headwinds.

Performance drivers: The fund returned −0.62% for the month. The equity sleeve was the primary detractor, as technology holdings (22.8% of the portfolio) faced profit-taking after a strong run. However, the high-yield allocation provided a cushion through steady coupon income, and select convertible bond positions in the healthcare and industrials sectors contributed positively through favorable conversion dynamics.

Positioning & outlook: We maintain our balanced three-sleeve approach (approximately one-third each in equities, high-yield bonds, and convertibles). Looking ahead, we see the current yield environment as attractive for income generation, while the equity allocation provides upside participation. We have modestly increased duration in the high-yield sleeve to lock in yields at current levels and remain constructive on US corporate credit quality.

Opportunities & Risks

Key factors to consider before investing

Opportunities

  • Attractive potential returns combining equities, high-yield bonds, and convertible bonds
  • Access to the US capital market — the world’s deepest and most liquid
  • Broad diversification across hundreds of securities reduces single-name risk
  • Additional return potential from active security analysis and tactical allocation
  • Currency gains possible for non-USD investors in unhedged share classes

Risks

  • All three asset classes may be subject to volatility; fund unit price may fluctuate significantly
  • US market underperformance could disproportionately impact the fund
  • High-yield bonds carry higher credit risk; defaults could impact returns
  • Success of active management and security selection is not guaranteed
  • Currency losses possible in unhedged share classes for non-USD investors
