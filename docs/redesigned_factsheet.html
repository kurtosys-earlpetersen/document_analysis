Manager Commentary & Market Context

Monthly insights from the investment team

Market environment: February saw a modest pullback in US equities as investors digested mixed economic signals. The S&P 500 retreated amid persistent inflation readings and trade policy uncertainty. HY spreads widened slightly but remained near historical tights, reflecting resilient corporate fundamentals.

Performance drivers: The fund returned −0.62%. The equity sleeve was the primary detractor as technology holdings (22.8%) faced profit-taking. The HY allocation provided a cushion through steady coupon income, and select convertible positions in healthcare and industrials contributed positively through favorable conversion dynamics.

Positioning & outlook: We maintain our balanced three-sleeve approach (~1/3 each). We see the current yield environment as attractive for income generation. We have modestly increased duration in the HY sleeve to lock in yields and remain constructive on US corporate credit quality.

Opportunities & Risks

Key factors to consider before investing

Opportunities
  • Attractive potential returns combining equities, HY bonds, and convertible bonds
  • Access to the US capital market — deepest and most liquid globally
  • Broad diversification across hundreds of securities reduces single-name risk
  • Additional return potential from active security analysis and tactical allocation
  • Currency gains possible for non-USD investors in unhedged share classes
Risks
  • All three asset classes may be subject to volatility; fund price may fluctuate significantly
  • US market underperformance could disproportionately impact the fund
  • HY bonds carry higher credit risk; defaults could impact returns
  • Success of active management and security selection is not guaranteed
  • Currency losses possible in unhedged share classes for non-USD investors
